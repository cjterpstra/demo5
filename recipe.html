Haluski Recipe

HISTORY

My grandmother always cooks me Haluski when I come over. This recipe has been passed down for generations and is my favorite Hungarian dish. My aunt and my grandparents are Hungarian and love to share the cultured food with me. The background of Haluski can be debatable, but can be claimed in some form or another by the Polish, Slovaks, Ukrainians, and Hungarians. Haluski is served during the winter months along with soups and stews. Haluski is a simple dish that contains cabbage and onions fried in butter, then tossed with egg noodles, salt, and pepper. This dish is one that I look forward to, and you should too.

DESCRIPTION

Haluski is a Hungarian dish that is beloved by many Hungarians. It can vary many different ingredients to your liking.

Ingredients

  • 1 (16 ounce) package medium-wide egg noodles
  • 1 cup butter, divided
  • 2 large onions, chopped
  • 2 small heads cabbage, cored and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

Instructions

Step

  1. Preheat the oven to 300 degrees F (150 degrees C).
  2. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil. Stir in egg noodles and return to a boil. Cook noodles uncovered, stirring occasionally, until tender but slightly firm, about 5 minutes. Drain well and set aside.
  3. Melt 1/2 cup butter in a large skillet over medium-low heat. Add onions; cook and stir until translucent, 5 to 10 minutes.
  4. Add cabbage and remaining 1/2 cup butter to onions; cook and stir until cabbage is softened but not browned, 5 to 8 minutes. Season with salt and black pepper.
  5. Place cooked noodles and cabbage mixture in a large roasting pan; stir gently to combine. Sprinkle with more salt and black pepper, if desired.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown on top, 30 to 40 minutes.

RECIPE TIPS

  • For extra flavor, you can add 3 strips cooked crumbled bacon to cabbage and noodle mixture before baking. This recipe makes a large amount, so share it with family and friends who have never tried it!
